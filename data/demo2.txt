Sales Rep: Hi Sarah, thanks for taking the time to chat today. I’d love to understand more about how your HVAC business currently handles incoming calls.

Client: Sure. Our company is called CoolAir HVAC Services. We primarily do residential HVAC installation, repairs, and seasonal maintenance.

Sales Rep: Sounds good. How many technicians do you have on the team?

Client: About ten technicians currently.

Sales Rep: And how are incoming customer calls handled right now?

Client: During the day our receptionist answers the calls and schedules appointments.

Sales Rep: And after hours?

Client: That’s where things get tricky. After 6 PM the phone system switches to voicemail.

Sales Rep: Do customers still call during those hours?

Client: Yes, especially in the summer when air conditioners fail during heat waves.

Sales Rep: I can imagine that’s stressful for customers.

Client: Definitely. Sometimes people leave urgent messages saying their AC stopped working and their house is extremely hot.

Sales Rep: And how does your team respond to those calls?

Client: Our on-call technician checks voicemail periodically and calls back.

Sales Rep: Would you prefer those calls to be routed directly?

Client: Yes. Ideally if it's an emergency, the call should be transferred to the on-call technician immediately.

Sales Rep: What would count as an emergency for your business?

Client: Heating failures in winter or cooling failures during extreme heat.

Sales Rep: And non-emergency calls?

Client: Those could just be recorded and scheduled for the next business day.

Sales Rep: And your business hours?

Client: Monday through Friday, 7 AM to 6 PM.
